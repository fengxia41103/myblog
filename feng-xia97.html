Cart ahead of the horse

Bumped into an article by the Google news directory, "A fiasco in the making? As the coronavirus pandemic takes hold, we are making decisions without reliable data", and its follow up discussion that "Scientists who express different views on Covid-19 should be heard, not demonized". I find them very interesting because a recent event between me and Noah had echoed a similar dilemma — can we know ahead of time? or in other words, are we constantly putting a cart in front of a horse?

Both questions sound pretty, stupid. Any common sense would say NO to both — no we can't know ahead of time (as we often heard no one has a crystal ball), and certainly it's a bad sign if the cart is put in front the horse (as my current project is perceived to be). But, if the answers are so obvious, why do they even pop up, all the time, and worthwhile to discuss? Let me first start the even w/ Noah.
